Designing a conceptual rocket ship that uses coal as fuel for a round trip to Mars presents numerous challenges, primarily due to coal's inefficiency, weight, and environmental impact compared to conventional rocket fuels. However, for the sake of this exercise, let's explore how such a system might be theoretically designed.

### 1. Propulsion System
- **Hybrid Rocket Engine**: 
  - Utilize a hybrid propulsion system where coal is used in combination with another oxidizer. This might include combining pulverized coal with liquid oxygen (LOX) to create a hybrid combustion process.
  - Coal would need to be pulverized into fine dust to efficiently mix with the oxidizer and achieve higher combustion efficiency.
  - Incorporate a thrust chamber with materials capable of withstanding the high temperatures generated by coal combustion.

- **Gasification Process**: 
  - Implement onboard gasification technology to convert coal into syngas (a mixture of hydrogen, carbon monoxide, and other hydrocarbons). The syngas can then be combusted to generate thrust.
  - This process would involve reactors designed to handle the thermal and pressure demands of gasification in space.

### 2. Fuel Storage
- **Coal Storage Tanks**:
  - Design modular coal storage tanks that can manage the dense, heavy nature of coal while minimizing weight. These tanks would need to be pressure-regulated and insulated to handle coal dust and prevent combustion prior to intended use.

- **Syngas Storage**:
  - Equip the craft with high-strength, heat-resistant tanks to store syngas generated during the flight, ensuring that the system can switch to a cleaner and more controlled fuel source mid-flight.

### 3. Safety Measures
- **Dust Management**:
  - Implement robust filtration and containment systems to manage coal dust, preventing potential dust explosions and ensuring the safety of on-board systems.
  - Include systems to minimize static electricity and isolate different sections of the propulsion system.

- **Thermal Management**:
  - Design advanced cooling systems for both the rocket's nozzle and the walls of the combustion chambers to manage the high temperatures resulting from coal combustion.
  - Use heat-resistant materials like reinforced carbon-carbon composites to prevent structural failures.

- **Environmental Controls**:
  - Integrate carbon capture and storage technology to manage emissions during Earth's atmosphere exit and entry phases, reducing the craft's carbon footprint and limiting pollution.

### 4. Additional Considerations
- **Weight Management**:
  - Optimize the mass distribution and structural design to accommodate the heavy weight of coal and the necessary auxiliary systems (e.g., gasifiers, storage tanks).
  - Consider additional weight-related complications, such as increased fuel requirements for launching and landing.

- **Redundancy and Fail-Safes**:
  - Ensure redundancy in all critical systems, such as propulsion and cooling, to mitigate risks associated with coal's unpredictable energy yield compared to liquid fuels.
  - Implement multiple emergency systems for abort scenarios, especially during the ascent and descent phases.

This conceptual design faces significant practical and safety challenges that make it largely unfeasible by current standards. Alternative and more efficient technologies such as liquid hydrogen/oxygen or advanced ion drives would be far more suitable for an interplanetary mission. The environmental, logistical, and engineering obstacles underscore the necessity of using more advanced and sustainable technologies for space exploration.
